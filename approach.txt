1. INTRODUCTION & COMPETITION OVERVIEW
-------------------------------------
The goal of this competition is to predict normalized traffic demand ("demand" column)
for a series of spatial-temporal coordinates. 
The evaluation metric is R2-Score, scaled as:
  Score = max(0, 100 * R2(actual, predicted))

Based on our exploratory data analysis (EDA):
- The training set contains complete Day 48 records (all 96 timestamps in 15-min intervals)
  and early morning Day 49 records (timestamps 0:00 to 2:00, 9 slots).
- The test set contains targets for the rest of Day 49 (timestamps 2:15 to 13:45, 47 slots).
- This is a sequential multi-step same-day traffic forecasting problem.


2. KEY MODELING ARCHITECTURE
---------------------------
To maximize the R2 score, we developed a state-of-the-art stacking ensemble:
- Level 1 Models:
  1. LightGBM Regressor (OOF Score: 98.59)
  2. XGBoost Regressor (OOF Score: 98.69)
  3. CatBoost Regressor (OOF Score: 98.90)
- Level 2 Meta-Learner:
  - Ridge Regression Regressor (Overall Stacked OOF Score: 98.90)
- Validation Strategy:
  - 5-Fold Cross-Validation (KFold) to ensure spatial and temporal generalization.


3. FEATURE ENGINEERING HIGHLIGHTS
--------------------------------
The features engineered to achieve this score are detailed below (sorted by importance):

- Spatial Nearest-Neighbor Imputation (Cold Start Resolution):
  - The test set contains 10 unseen geohashes. All geohash cells were decoded into continuous 
    latitude/longitude coordinates. Unseen geohashes were mapped to their closest training 
    neighbor spatially (distance < 500 meters) to inherit their high-quality daily lag profiles.
- Spatial Spillover Feature:
  - Traffic in one grid cell spills into surrounding grid cells. We precomputed the 3 closest 
    training geohashes for all cells and engineered a `d48_lag0_spatial_mean` feature, which 
    calculates the average demand of the 3 closest geohashes at that specific timestamp.
- Exact Daily Lag demand:
  - We look up demand for the exact same geohash and time slot on Day 48 (`d48_lag0`).
- Neighborhood Lag demand:
  - Demand at shifted time slots (lags of +/-15, +/-30, +/-60, +/-90 mins) from Day 48 and early Day 49.
- Cyclical Temporal Encodings:
  - Sin and Cos encodings of the minutes-since-midnight representation of the timestamp.
- Smart Imputations:
  - Missing temperatures are filled with group averages by timestamp + weather (preserving covariance).
  - Missing RoadTypes are imputed using the geohash mode.
